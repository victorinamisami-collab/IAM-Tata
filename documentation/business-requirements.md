
# Business Requirements Document: IAM Strategy

## 1. Project Background
This document outlines the Identity and Access Management (IAM) governance framework designed during the Tata Consultancy Services Cybersecurity Job Simulation. The objective was to audit, restructure, and align corporate user access permissions with enterprise security goals.

## 2. Strategic Business Alignment
To protect corporate assets while ensuring smooth business operations, the IAM framework enforces three core business rules:
* **Risk Reduction:** Minimize the corporate attack surface by eliminating unnecessary user privileges.
* **Operational Continuity:** Ensure employees have exactly the tools they need to perform their jobs without administrative delays.
* **Regulatory Compliance:** Maintain clear documentation of user access levels to satisfy external security audits.

## 3. Core IAM Security Principles Applied
* **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP):** Users are granted only the minimum access necessary to complete their specific job functions.
* **Separation of Duties (SoD):** Critical tasks are divided among multiple roles to prevent fraud, unauthorized changes, or single points of failure.
* **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):** Permissions are assigned to specific job roles (e.g., Consultant, Auditor, Administrator) rather than individual employees.
